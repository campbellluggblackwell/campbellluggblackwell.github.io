211 Tenth St., N.E.,

Washington, D. C.,

April 8th, 1926.

My dear Cousins:-

I have been asked to send a letter for this years [sic] "cousin book." I had the extreme pleasure of being entertained in the homes of many who belong to the Cousin Clan. This I have thoroughly enjoyed as each one has seemed to try in their own way to outdo the others for my comfort.

As I continued to meet other members of this organization, I feel that fortune has surely been mine to have made it possible for me to become a member of this wonderful Campbell family.

Our home is made up of four members namely: my good wife Mary, and her sister Florence Bosard, my son Austin and myself.

A hearty welcome is extended to any of the Cousins who may find it possible to pay us a visit. Although I am only an "In-law", the family tie is very strong.

Sincerely yours,

COUSIN A. B. SNAVELY.
