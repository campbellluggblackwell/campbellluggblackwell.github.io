Campbells of Nelson, PA

[Mostly family news in this 1970 letter from 75 year old Elizabeth HOYT Walker in Sarasota, FL to her sister, Jessica HOYT Thompson, in Binghamton, NY - wbt]


<

Saturday Night March 21

 Dear Jess, Walter and all,

Have been trying to write some letters for days, but haven't been in the mood.

Finally got a frame for my picture, its gift next to the picture. Then the rest a dull black like the desk.

This has been our month for company. Blanch [sic] Cumberson1 was here for four days, then Agnes & John Smith came & were here 11 days. I'm just getting rested up.

Joanne & Terri came over yesterday morning & spent the

1. Apparently Blanche CRANDALL Cumberson (1872 - 1974) was a friend  of Betty & Roy and an acquaintance of Jessica & Walter from when they were living in E. Brookfield or W. Winfield in Madison Co., NY shortly after their marriages.

2

day with us. Joanne cut out a dress and we worked on it. Today I've finished it all up except putting in the hem.2 Their house is coming along fast now should be in it by April 15th or before.

Did Ruth send you a page out of the Elmira Telegram with old pictures of Nelson? the one she sent me, we got a big kick out of it.

So the Hanks3 are moving to Halstead. Lou Eddy's sister lived there for years, can't think what her name was

2. Betty's mother was a trained seamstress.

3. Her nephew, Henry G. Thompson, & family.

3

So far guess Dave's work has been O.K. (I guess) he works for a firm in Norwich. Most of his work is in the state, goes to Cooperstown, Oneonta & all over. Think Evelyn is enjoying teaching at the nursing school, & Scotty goes with her every day & loves it.

I remember how homesick Joanne was when they first moved down here. Now don't think she remembers much about it. Was so tickled that Moe got a regents scholarship, I don't wonder Anne is proud

4

of him.

Isn't it nice the boys4 are enjoying Masonry, and each other.

I'll send you Dorothy's letter, an [sic] she wrote it after her birthday.

Roy isn't good. I've felt so uncomfortable thought I'd hospital next month, but won't go unless he gets to feeling better. he has lost a lot of weight, but he won't go to a doctor.

Ruth Green had a party Wed. night. there were 11 Winfielders there.5 They served drinks then had a nice dinner & he hasn't been able to eat since.

4. Betty's son Dave & Jessica's eldest son, Walter, Jr. In addition to being Masons, they fished together and loved nature. It was always a pleadsure to spend time with Dave.

5. Residents (or former residents) of West Winfield, NY where Betty & lived as a young couple.

5

doesn't feel like doing anything. wrote David. thought if anything should happen to him they should know.Told them not to mention anything in their letters. I don't want him to know I'm worried.

Blanch [sic] Cumberson thought he had failed a lot since we were up North this summer.

We are having lovely weather now. 70° to 80° every day. Hope you are both feeling better and stay that way.

Love to everyone
Betty
