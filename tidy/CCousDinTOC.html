1. The Campbell Cousins Reunions were open to anyone, but were for the descendant of Joseph and Mary Harper Campbell and their Lugg and Blackwell relatives. At their peak had 200 - 300 attendees. They ran from 1893 to 1942, resumed in 2001, and moved to videoconferencing in 2020.
The Campbell Cousins Dinners were by invitation only. They began in 1909 and were intended for the 30 first cousins (grandchildren of Joseph and Ann Clinch Campbell) and for others invited by the hosting couple of that year. All 30 were born in or near Nelson, Tioga Co., PA and had all at least laid eyes on each other before some moved to other parts of the country. Most stayed in touch and those in Tioga Co. and adjacent Steuben and Chemung Counties were close emotionally, as well as geographically. In their later years, as death thinned their ranks, they included the next generation in the dinners, but by then the families had scattered all over the country, the second cousins didn't know each other well, and the Cousins Dinners came to an end.
