Campbells/Luggs/Blackwells of Nelson, PA Virtual Scrapbook

[Below is a transcription of what apparently was an insert for the Order of Service for the Nelson Community Church --- or possibly a transcription from the original. The copy I typed this from bore the inscription "Tho't maybe you'd like to know this!" I'm not sure who sent it to me, perhaps Cousin Ande RICE Tubbs. The reason it would have interested me that it mentions: my ggf, Rev. Henry Goodrich, who was pastor there when it was a Methodist Church before the building was moved from the South Side; my grandmother, Frances GOODRICH Hoyt; and my mother's 1st cousin, Ruth TUBBS Krise. It also mentions Campbell (also Lugg) Cousin Burton R. Rice, his wife, Clairbel BOYCE Rice, and their children: Eileen RICE Shepherd Harris, Dortha RICE Davenport, & Robert W. Rice. Moreover, it also mentions a group of our Blackwell Cousins (that particular branch also being Luggs).    --- Transcribed and annotated Feb 17, 2013 by Wm. B. Thompson]

Service for the Dedication of Memorials

MEMORIAL PLAQUE purchased from Building Funds.

I. GIFTS OF MONEY
Given in memory of:      Russell Gleason    Emily Heckert

                                       Robert Kirk          Clair & Grace Learn
                                       Archie Preston     Waldo Swan1
                                       Tres West
Labor money returned to church by Bing Hughes in memory of his father:
                                       Roy Sheldon Hughes

II. SPECIAL MEMORIALS

Carpeting in Fellowship Hall given in memory of : Mr. and Mrs. Henry Goodrich and
                                   Mrs. Frances G. Hoyt by Ruth Krise, Knoxville, PA

Reconstruction of Church Sign in memory of Burton & Claribel Rice by:
                                    Mr. and Mrs. Eileen Harris & family             Mr. and Mrs. Robert Rice
                                    Mr. & Mrs. George Davenport

(Original Sign given in memory of Billy Bailey)

Sanctuary Clock given in memory of Judith Marshall Farmelo and her father, Stanley Farmelo by: Eileen Harris (Judith's best friend)

    Guests attending Service:       Mr. & Mrs. Glenn Neal (foster parents of Judith)
                                                   Mr. & Mrs. John Rice (cousins)

Enoch Blackwell Family2 Trust given by:    Enoch Charles & Lena Blackwell
                Enoch & Mary Blackwell            Enoch Arden & Cora Blackwell
                Enoch & Caroline Blackwell       Enoch Delivan & Theresa Blackwell
                Enoch Scott Blackwell

III. OTHER MEMORIALS (to be put in book)

Baptismal given in memory of hteir (sic) parents, Mr. & Mrs. Ben Manley by:
                Mr. & Mrs. Harold Manley, Mr. & Mrs. Gordon Manley and
                Mr. & Mrs. Calvin Orr
Sound System given in memory of his wife, Effie Heysham Antonio by John Antonio

MINISTER: We present this memorial to be dedicated to the glory of the almighty God,
and for service in this church in living memory of _______.

ACCEPTING MEMORIAL: We accept this gift as a sacred trust, and shall guard it reverently,
in honor of the faithful and devoted life in whose memory it is given.

MINISTER: In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, we dedicate this
memorial to the glory of God, and in memory of his servant ________. The
memory of the righteous is ever blessed.

Give unto the Lord the glory due unto his name: worship the Lord in the beauty of holiness.
Let us Pray.

Almighty God our heavenly Father,without whom no words or works of ours have meaning, but who dost accept the gifts of our hands as the tokens of our devotion: Grant thy blessing upon us as we dedicate this gift to thy glory. May this memorial which we now dedicate be an enduring witness before all they people of the faithful service of thy servant. May our lives, being consecrated unto thy service, be joined with the faithful ones into that building which groweth unto a holy temple in the Lord. Amen.

1. Waldo "Wad" Swan, who operated Nelson's general store, and his big brother, Curt, were Nelson institutions --- wbt.

2. There were so many people named "Enoch Blackwell" that it makes it hard to be sure which ones are being referred to if no middle name is given.There also are many "Mary Blackwell"s and several "Caroline Blackwell"s. Where no middle name is given, I have guessed at which seemed most likely --- wbt.
