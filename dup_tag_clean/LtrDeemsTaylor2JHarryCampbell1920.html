CAMPBELLs, LUGGs, & BLACKWELLs of Nelson, PA

[Image provided courtesy of J. Harry Campbell's granddaughter, Barbara Righter. Thank you Barbara.]


25th Oct 1920

Dear Mr. Campbell,

Thank you for your generous note of the 22nd. I'm so glad you are using "The Messenger"; it's my favorite of the three1, and you're the first singer who has yet been able to "see" it.2 I shall give myself the pleasure of running in on Thursday to hear it and you.

Sincerely yours,
Deems Taylor
189 1/2 East 83d St
New York

1. In 1920 Taylor's Opus 13, Three Songs, was published. They were for voice and piano: "The Rivals," "Songs for Lovers" and "The Messenger." The latter was an art song in which the singer asks a bee to give a message to the singer's sweetheart.

2. That is, Harry was the first singer "to get it" -- to understand and appreciate the song.
