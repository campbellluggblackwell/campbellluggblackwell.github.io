Campbells of Nelson, PA

Nelson, Pa.      Aug 15, 1912

Campbell Reunion Society met on the High School House Grounds on the morning of Aug. 15. The usual good dinner was served at 1 o'clock. After which the meeting called to order by Pres. followed by The Campbell Reunion Song.1 Prayer by Rev. Barnard.

Address of Welcome was given by J. E. Hazlett and response by Mr. Raymond Steele. This was followed by an excellent original poem by Mrs. M. B. Seely.

A short talk by Prof. George Cass2 followed reading of the minutes by Miss Eva Hooker.

Regrets were read from
Dr. Harry Horton   Baker, Oregon
C.H. Congdon       Chicago, Ill.
James Ellison        Marengo, Ill.
W. E. Selph           N. Y.
& Emma Buck       Watkins, N. Y.

Officers elected for 1913 were
M.B. Seely - President
T.C. Campbell - Vice Presdent
Clyde Campbell - Sec'y
M.B. Seely - Treasurer

Collection $7.80 leaving $6.09 in treasury after paying expenses.

Meetng adjourned.

Eva Hooker Sec'y.

1. Words by Mabel Shipman Shaw. Sung to the tune of "Marching Through Georgia."

2. George Cass (1879 - 1954) was not a "blood relative," but he was connected. His mother's sister was Eva (Baxter) Foster Hazlett. Also, he was a grandson of Hon. Geo. H. Baxter, whose first wife was Sarah Clarissa Campbell. The first Campbell Reunion was held in a picnic grove on Judge Baxter's farm.
