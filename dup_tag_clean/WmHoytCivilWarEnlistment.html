VOLUNTEER ENLISTMENT

STATE OF                              TOWN OF

             Penna                                   Farmington     

I,  William Hoyt       born in   Fairfield  , in the State of     Connecticut    , aged   33  , years, and by occupation a   farmer    , Do hereby acknowledge to have volunteered this  first   day of   September  , 1864, to serve as a Soldier in the Army of the United States of America, for a period of THREE one YEARS, unless sooner discharged by proper authority: Do also agree to accept such bounty, pay, rations, and clothing as are, or may be, established by law for volunteers. And I,   William Hoyt     do solemnly swear, that I will bear true faith and allegiance to the United States of America, and that I will serve them1 honestly and faithfully against all their enemies or opposers whomsoever, and that I will observe and obey the orders of the President of the United States, and the orders of the officers appointed over me, according to the Rules and Articles of War.

Sworn and subscribed to, at Farmington      ⎫
             this first day of September 1864      ⎬   Wm. Hoyt 
Before        R. T. Wood                               ⎭
                 Lieut. & Muster offr

I CERTIFY, ON HONOR, That I carefully examined the above-named Volunteer, agreeably to the General Regulations of the Army, and that, in my opinion, he is free from all bodily defects and mental infirmity, which would in any way disqualify him from performing the duties of a soldier.

   R. Wastin   

Examining Surgeon

I CERTIFY, ON HONOR, That I have minutely examined the Volunteer ________________________
previously to his enlistment, and that, he was entirely sober when he enlisted,2 that to the best of my judgement and belief, he is of lawful age; and that, in accepting him as duly qualified to perform the duties of an able-bodied soldier. I have strictly observed the Regulations which govern the recruiting service. This soldier has  Light  eyes,  Light  hair,  Light  complexion, is  5  feet  10  inches high. & is credited to Farmington 18th Congressional D, Pa .

_________ Regiment of _______________________ Volunteers

Recruiting, officer

Mustered into the service of the United States, for three one years or during the war, from date of enlistment, in Company H   207 Regiment of Penna Volunteers, on the 3 day of   Sept     , 1864 at Camp Curtin Pa.

   Simon Snyder   

   Cap. 3 U S Inf   

   M. O
