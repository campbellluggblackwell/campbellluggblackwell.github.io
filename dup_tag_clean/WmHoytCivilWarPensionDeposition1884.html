DEPOSITION "A"

Case of William Hoyt, No. 328.722

On this   14th   day of   Nov.  , 1884, at                                , County of   Lancaster  , State of     Pa    , before me,   E. W. Johnson  , a Special Examiner of the Pension Office, personally appeared   Washington Bury   , who, being by me first duly sworn to answer truly all interrogatories propounded to him during this Special Examination of aforesaid person, claim, deposes and says: _____

Q: What is your age, occupation and P.O.address.                       

A: My age is 46 years. occupation practicing physician. And my P.O. address is East Petersburg. Lancaster Co. Pa                   .

Q: How long have you been in the practice of medicine.                

A: Since the Spring of 57.                                                       

Q: Were you in the army and if so what was your rank.         

A: Yes sir, I served. I was Surg. of the 207 Pa Vols             

Q: Were you acquainted with a man by the name of William Hoyt who belonged to Co. H, of the above mentioned Regt.

A: Yes, sir, I was. He was one [of my] orderlys [sic] serving most of the time he was in the service. And especially during the latter part of his service.

Lancaster, Pa Nov. 15/84

Hon. W. W. Dudley
Commissioner of Pensions
Washington, D.C.

Sir:

I have the honor herewith to return the original papers with my report in case No. 325.722 of William Hoyt late private of Co. H, 207 Pa Vols, whose P.O, address is Nelson, Tioga Co., Pa.

This claim was sent to me to take the Deposition of Washing [sic] Bury, who was the Surg. of that above named Regt.

An abstract of his evidence is contained in the following.

Summary.

Washington Burg (Surg.)1 Testified was not acquainted with claimant prior to his enlistment. I have him detailed to act as one of my orderlys [sic] during the time he was afflicted with Rheumatism which affected his entire system and especially his back and hips. And my impression is his disease was brought on by exposure. Rep[utation] Doubtful.2

1. Deposition "A".

2. Page 2. Presumably it was Burg's reputation that E. W. Johnson was characterizing as "doubtful".
