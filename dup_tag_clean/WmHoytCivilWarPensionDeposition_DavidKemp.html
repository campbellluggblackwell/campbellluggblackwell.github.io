Campbells of Nelson, PA

David Cook Kemp and Ashibell C. Wheeler's Deposition1 on William Hoyt's Application for a Civil War Pension

GENERAL AFFIDAVIT

State of Pennsylvania

County of Tioga

In the matter of                             William Hoyt   a                       

(Character and number of Claim)

      Private of Co "H" 207th Regt Pa Vol Inf                       

(Full name and relationship to claimant and name and service of soldier)

Personally came before me a              Notary Public                   in and for

(Justice. Notary, Judge, Clerk, or Deputy Clerk)

aforesaid county and state,              David C. Kemp and Ashbell Wheeler                

(Here write the name of affiant, of of each affiant,together with Age, Residence, and Post-Office address)

      both residents of Farmington Tioga County                       

      Pennsylvania                       

persons of lawful age, who, being duly sworn, declare in relation to the aforesaid claim as follows:

 We have been acquainted with the above applicant for more than twenty years, & knew him personally before his enlistment. That at that date we considered s sound and able bodied man & free from Rheumatism or any other illnesses. That since his discharge we have known him personally & been near neighbors of his & know of our own personal that he is not an able bodied man nor capable of doing active manual labor, that his business is farming & and has been since we have known him, & to the best of our belief he cannot perform more than one half of the labors he could do before he entered the service. 

                                                                                                                                                                   

 (The cause of his not being an "able bodied man and not capable of doing active manual labor since his discharge" is because he is and has been from the time of his discharge up to the present time troubled by Rheumatism in his hip and leg.) 

 D. C. Kemp           

 A. C. Wheeler        

                                                                                                                                                            

 The above addition included in parentheses from the words "The Oath" was sworn and deposed before me this 29th day of April 1882. Charles M. Ward Notary Public 

 They   further declare that   they have no interest in said case and   are  not concerned in its prosecution.

Signature of    D C Kemp   

each Affirmate. A C Wheeler

1. David Kemp gave another deposition on this in 1884.
