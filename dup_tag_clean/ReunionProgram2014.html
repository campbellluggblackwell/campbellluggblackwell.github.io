Our great Campbell reunion.

CHORUS: Hurrah! Hurrah! We sing in praise of thee,
Our kindred brave, and land of liberty.
We shout the chorus far and wide, it rings from

And settled Beecher’s Island when the land was strange and new;
Keep honoring those settlers, and the red, the white and blue,
At our Campbell reunion.

Our county is Tioga in the glorious land of Penn,
And ours a noble ancestry of brave, free-hearted men,
We pledge ourselves in friendship's bond and hope to meet again,
At our Campbell reunion.

Our circle can’t be broken, tho some have left too soon
Let’s keep our new technologies united and in tune
And stay in touch with Campbell kin long after our afternoon
In a Campbell reunion.

A Brief History of the Campbell Reunions

The first Reunion was held in Nelson at the "instigation" of Phebe Campbell Hoyt in
1893 in Nelson. The reunions continued annually until the 50th in 1942, when the
combination of deaths & geographic scattering brought them to an end.

In 2000, fifty cousins held a planning meeting at Tioga Lake Park, reinstituted the Reunion Association, and made plans for the 51st reunion. It was held in Nelson the following year. We now hold them in even numbered years and hope that the reunions will continue on a regular basis, well into the future.
