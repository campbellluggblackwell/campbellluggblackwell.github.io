NINTH ANNUAL REUNION

TO BE HELD AT

NELSON, PA., THURSDAY, AUGUST 14, 1902

YOURSELF and FAMILY ARE MOST CORDIALLY INVITED TO BE PRESENT
BY SPECIAL REQUEST of the OFFICERS.

M. B. SEELY, Pres.                                 T.C. CAMPBELL, Secy.




[front cover---wbt]

Notes.

There will be a meeting of all the committees held on Tuesday, July 15, at 1 o'clock P.M. at the home of Joseph D. Hoyt in Nelson, Pa., to complete arrangements for the properly carrying out the designs of this organization and arrange to make this, our ninth annual gathering, the largest and most enjoyable one we have ever had.

Every member of the several committees (except the reception committee) is expected to be present at this meeting. There are things it is necessary we should confer together regarding, in order to make the reunion a success.

The first person named on each committee is the chairman of that committee.

All committeemen will receive one of these invitations as soon as they are printed. All others will receive their invitations direct from the committee on invitations.

SUGGESTIONS.

The Campbells are coming, Ha, Ha, Ha, Ha.

Here is [sic] a few of the things they are expected to bring.
A Basket of Refreshments.  And the Babies.
And a Smile.                       A Drinking Cup.
A Knife, Fork and Spoon.    And a disposition to have a good time.

Every one is requested to be on the grounds at 12 o'clock., noon. It is intended to have the dinner served at 12:30 sharp, on account of those who come from a distance and necessarily have had an early breakfast, and to give time for the program, business meeting and exchange a few friendly greetings.

It is earnestly requested that each one who is called upon by the committee on entertainment respond promptly with the part assigned them.
