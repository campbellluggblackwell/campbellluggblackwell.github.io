Campbells of Nelson, PA

[2003 Letter from Marian VAN DUSEN McBurnie (1923 - 2010) to William B. Thompson]


Click HERE to see the rest of the handwritten pages.

2714 19th Ave W.

Bradenton, Fl. 34005

December 7, 2003

Dear 3rd Cousin Bill,

Glad to hear have some more relatives -- I was wondering how you know Lula Moore. The only one by that name that I know, is my husband, Gordon's, niece that lives near Spokane, Wash. Is that the same person?1

I've never been to a Campbell Reunion, but every other year we do have a Hall-Van Dusen one,2 usually at my sister's daughter's. Those are always fun. I don't think we have discovered any real criminals either!3

My Dad, Ross Van Dusen, died at the age of 31 yrs. of spinal meningitis. I was only 8 months old at the same time, so I have no recollection of him at all. Also I was only 2 yrs old when mom married Otis Tubbs.

1. I didn't know Lula Moore. Probably I saw a post from her mentioning Gordon on a message board. At the time that was the primary way for genealogy hobbists to communicate.

2. Marian's letter was in response to one I sent as a "first contact" letter inviting her to the 2004 Campbell Reunion, saying "Hi" and introducing myself and the reunion.

3. Her mention of criminals was in response to a comment I often made in first contact letters about so far not having discovered any horse thieves in the family tree.

II

This was a very brief marriage, & I have no recollection of him, either. However Johnny Tubbs (no relation)4 was the Step-Dad I remember. He was a great Dad, & he & Mom were married about 1929 (April (7th). I was 6 yrs. old at the time. I don't have their parents' names, but I know Johnny had a brother, Bill Tubbs, 2 sisters, Bess Seeley [sic], & Ellen Lockwood. Bess lived in Osceola & Ellen lived in Nelson on on a dairy farm. -- both deceased.

My sister Helen & I both out of Osceola & somewhere along the way, as we closed up my mom's house after her death, I think in Sept, of 1972 -- However I'm not sure of the date since the family Bible disappeared in all the moves we have made & all the information was recorded in that. I'm sorry I can't be more helpful, but if I find any of that information, I'll let you know.

My sister (only sister) is still living, born August 5, 1918, and is now in a retirement home apartment. She's very well and lives near her

4. Marian had no way of knowing, but John J. Tubbs and Otis B. Tubbs were related -- third cousins, connected two ways: both were great-great-grandchildren of Samuel & Sarah Dorrance Tubbs; and both were great-great-grandchildren of Ebenezer & Polly Cook Taylor.].

III

2 children, Linda & Ned. She was married to E. Francis Hall, son of Burr and Vilette Hall (also Osceola, Pa.)

I knew my uncle as Burr Van Dusen.5 He lived on a farm up out of Osceola.

My mother's only brother was Donald Steadman.6 He lived in Binghamton, N.Y. He passed away several years ago.

Is the William Van Dusen7 that lives here in Florida the son of William Van Dusen8 that worked for years in India, for Standard Oil? He had a sister named Sara [sic] Cardman,9 also deceased.

Here are some addresses that may help you:

Mrs. Helen R. Hall
554 Oak Oval
Messiah Village
Mechanicsburg, Pa. 17055-8409
Rel: (717)796-1677

5. Everyone referred to him as "Burr Van Dusen," but I had written Marion that he signed his WW1 & WW2 draft registration cards as "William Burr Van Dusen.".

6. See https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/98406061/donald-c_-steadman.

7. I no longer have a GEDCOM file that includes William, but yes he had a father William and a sister, Sarah VanDusen Cardman. Wm. Jr. and I corresponded some by e-mail--wbt.

8. I don't know whether or not he ever lived in India or worked for Standard Oil-wbt.

9. Sarah Elizabeth VanDUSEN Cardman, Born 1901 in Farmington Twp., Tioga Co., PA; died 1996 in Lakeland, FL.

IV

Helen's husband, Francis died Jan 12, 2001. My husband, Gordon passed away just 3 wks. later (on Francis' birthday) Feb 8th 2001.

Helen can tell you when they left Osceola. We left Osceola in Oct of 1944 after my husband was discharged from the Air Force (served 4½ years).10 We moved to Rhode Island where we raised potatoes.11 We lived there in West Kingston, R I until Oct. of 1972 -- moved to Florida where I still reside.

My daughter, Anita's address is:
[address redacted for privacy]

I do hope you can read my horrible handwriting -- my phone # (941)-747-6096.

I will be interested in hearing more about you -- Do have a very merry Christmas & a very Happy and healthy new year.--


My birthday is April 4, 1923
married on Nov. 18, 1943

Very best wishes,
Marian J. McBurnie

10.During WW2, most enlistments and draft obligations were extended "for the duration" -- i.e. until the war ended. Perhaps Gordon was wounded and received a medical discharge. Or, since he was in the Army Air Corps he may have been on a flight crew and released because of the number of combat missions flown or hours of combat flight time. They used complex formulas that differed for fighter and bomber units, theater of war, and year that governed flight crew rotation, and as the manpower supply increased, influenced release.

11. He grew up potato farming in Aroostook Co., ME.
