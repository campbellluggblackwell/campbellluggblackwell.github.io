SYMPHONY SOCIETY OF NEW YORK

Mr. WALTER DAMROSCH, Conductor

AEOLIAN HALL NEW YORK

Sept. 12. 1914

Mr. F. W. Haensel1

Aeolian Hall

Dear Mr. Haensel:

It gives me great pleasure to recommend unreservedly the young tenor, Mr. John H. Campbell. He has a wonderful voice, well placed, and he sings with an enthusiam and musical intelligence, which, alas, is very rare among singers, and especially among tenor singers. You remember what Von Bulow said, that "a tenor is not a voice, but a disease." Well, Mr. Campbell is a notable exception.

Sincerely yours,
WALTER DAMROSCH

1. Fitzhugh W. Haensel, of the firm Haensel & Jones, was the manager of bookings for this and other venues. He apparently was an impresario and a celebriity.- wbt
